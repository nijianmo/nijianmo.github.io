Jianmo Ni

Computer Science and Engineering, University of California San Diego

I am a PhD student at University of California San Diego since September 2016. Currently, I am rotating in the System Energy Efficiency Lab with Prof. Tajana Rosing.
My researches span widely in energy-efficiently computing, including Internet of Things, embedded system, mobile system and smart grid. I am also interested in exploring and implementing optimization algorithms like graph theory, game-theory and machine learning.

Before I came to UC San Diego, I have obtained the M.E. degree from Shanghai Jiao Tong University and Waseda University at 2016 and 2014, and the B.S. degree from Shanghai Jiao Tong University at 2013. I have been involved in several themes and projects related with high level synthesis and smart grid.
More recently, I have been working on context-aware computing by leveraging large scale of context infomation collected from sensors and machine learning algorithms to perform activity recognition and design control optimization algorithms.

Research Experience

  • Context-aware engine for IoT Applications 2016.09-present

    Developing a context-aware engine by leveraging various kinds of context data collected from IoT systems (eg. smart cities, smart grid). Accomodating machine learning algorithms (eg. regression, clustering, anomaly detection) to pre-process and analyze large scale data.

  • Smart Microgrid Energy Management System 2014.10-2015.10

    Established the energy management system for a micro-grid with PVs, WTs, Battery and PHEVs. Designed different control patterns for the distributed energy resources in the system. Implemented dispatch operation algorithms for increasing economic-efficiency and lowing power cost, maintaining the stability and reliability of the microgrid system. Developed an economic transaction scheme by taking advantage of direct trading of micro-grids, apply coalitional game theory to find optimal coalition structure.

  • Simultaneous Functional Unit and Register Binding for Interconnection Reduction 2014.04-2014.08

    Traversed the interconnection reduction problem into a min-cost max-flow by introducing edge-weight function to estimate the interconnection cost, perform simultaneous FU and register binding by finding flow paths and binding FUs and registers on the same path. Proposed a primal-dual based method to solve the problem min-cost max-flow problem and improved final result compared with conventional greedy algorithm.

  • Multiple Voltage Scheduling for Low Power Design 2013.4-2014.03

    Developed a scheduling scheme for multiple voltage scheduling under both timing and resource constraints aiming at minimize power consumption (NP hard problem). Proposed a coarse-grained tabu search by representing the vector of tabu search as the state of sub-problems which only consider the timing constraint, search from solution space to solution space, effectively avoid from getting into local optimal. Introduced heuristic function to estimate the solution of sub-problems and accelerated the searching speed.

  • Teaching Assistant at Waseda University 2013.10-2014.07

    Teaching Assistant of course LSI Design Automation and System LSI Software in IPS Graduate School of Waseda University
